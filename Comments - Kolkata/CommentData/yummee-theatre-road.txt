The rolls are to-die-for, with juicy meat or paneer wrapped in fresh laccha parathas and coated with a spicy sauce. The fried rice, chowmein and chilli/garlic chicken all rival quality restaurants - for a cheap stall, the quality really is astonishingly good. Perhaps best of all are the rates - a half plate of fried rice or one roll cost about 30-40 each, and one item is filling enough for a hungry diner. Pair it with a half-plate of chicken and you have a wonderful Chinese meal that sets you back less than INR 100. 
Yummee is more than just an affordable eatery for Xaverians - it's a new standard in budget meals. Several overpriced restaurants I know would do well to pick up cookery tips from here. No matter what you try, you'll be left with a warm full tummy and a sigh of satisfaction on your lips.

If I am nearby Short Street, nothing can keep me away from Yummee. Yes! The name of this place is perfectly justified. Situated at 3B Short Street near St. Xavier's College Back Gate, Yummee is a Chinese restaurant serving a wide range of Chinese as well as fast food preparations at reasonably low rate. Nothing much to talk about the ambience, Yummee is a small restaurant with limited outdoor seating arrangements. The ambience may disappoint many but when you will look at the menu card along with the rate chart, you will understand that this ambience merely matters as you are getting Mixed Chow@ only Rs. 50 and so on. Where on earth you will find such?

Now considering such a low price, many may doubt about the quality as I did when I was there for the first time. Truly speaking, my first experience has returned me to this place many times. It is true that preparations served here are not obviously like Mainland China or Jimmy's Kitchen but not too bad to swallow. The preparations are by and large mediocre. Whenever I am there, I prefer to have chow or rice preparations along with some side dish. One thing about side dish, I need to mention. The taste is fine and the item is also perfect when it is a veg preparation, especially paneer. But whenever it comes to chicken preparation, you will find very small pieces of chicken. The chicken balls in Chilli Chicken, Hong Kong Chicken etc are filled with corn flour coating with very small chicken stuffing. And one more thing, it is always better not to try any kind of soup here. It is better to rename soup as spicy boiled water. However the roll section is fine. The parathas are thick and are well stuffed with the fillings. So whenever I don't have a plan to sit and dine here, I prefer to have Chicken or Veg roll.
