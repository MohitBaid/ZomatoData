This is one moderately good place. Some of the dishes they serve are no where up to the mark but some can actually give you a pleasant surprise. 

They have a hodgepodge decor and the ambience is kinda odd. Like, I have never seen this place full with all the tables occupied. There's always only two-three tables occupied at the most. Feels a little strange to sit there and have a meal with more waiters than guests around. 

Service can get slow. And the waiters are moderately efficient but polite nevertheless. 

Food, as much as I have tried so far, is better than just good. 

Their Chicken Tandoori and Peshawari Murgh Tikka is surprisingly good. The meat is very tender and succulent and with flavours seeped into the core, it is actually better than the Tandoori Chicken served in some of the mainstream Mughlai cuisine restaurant chains.
Try these, do. :)

The Calcutta style Dum Biriyani is average, thick with spices. The price they charge for it isn't justified at all. 

They make beautiful, absolutely delightful Tandoori Rotis and Naans. I mean, so good that they actually deserve a mention here. The scrumptious aroma of the whole wheat flour baked rotis combined with the sight of butter dripping down the surface can surely elevate your hunger.

Patthar ke Gosht. It's one of the good Mutton main course dishes that they serve. They gravy is red and spicy with the right amount of smooth creaminess to delight your taste buds. The meat is generally cooked well, tender and falls right off the bones. 
Funny thing, some of their dishes can taste alike. For example, Patthar ke Gosht and Laal Mas, they have a nearly identical gravy with different garnishes. That's actually disappointing. 


At any rate, if you do come in here and order the Tandoori Chicken or the Peshawari Murgh, you won't regret it. I highly recommend those. And the bill doesn't burn a hole in your pocket either. :)
