A one-time hero of the Kolkata roll world, nowadays people avoid Baadshah because of the exorbitant price and horrible food quality. A month back, I was shopping in New market, and, feeling quite hungry, my friend and I decided to drop in Baadshah for a couple of rolls. The place was quite empty, although it was 2.30 p.m, supposedly peak time. That should have warned us.

However, we do tend to be stupid, so ordered two chicken rolls. As we were seated, I noted that there were little bits of food sticking to the sofa, so i was quite turned off. The place has a bar upstairs, so I saw a good number of men going upstairs, while eyeing me and my friend, who was a girl, like we were meat on display. I was very irritated by their attitude. 

The rolls came in, and they were quite small, and not piping hot, but lukewarm. Although the chicken was okay, both my friend and I hated the paratha which was too oily and smelled of rapeseed oil. Our bill was somewhere around 70, and the waiter actually looked at us disappointed when we did not add any tip. For two rolls, served in very dirty conditions, we did not see fit to tip.

Sorry Badshaah, we will not return.

Badshah, once famous for its rolls, is now a  shadow of its former self. Excessive adulation has caused it to venture into the world of sit-down dining, dealing a death-blow to its roll business. Its location makes it the ideal spot for people to drop in before, after or while shopping, hence, it has become complacent.

The Mutton Roll, which is usually the decider of quality in any roll shop, is quite bad. The quality of the meat is good, but that is about it. It is cooked badly and stuffed into a limp paratha with tons of oily onions. The paratha is also very small - two-thirds the size of rolls from other well-known shops  - Kusum and Hot Kati Roll being the most obvious ones.

Patrons who do not go into the air-conditioned dining space are invited to sit on a sofa covered with pieces of onion, strips of mutton and bits of paratha. I had to dust a spot before being able to sit there. The roll was handed to me quickly enough, but, as described earlier, it was very bad. At 35 rupees per roll, it was simply not worth it. Hence, it is a place best avoided.

Badshah in Lindsay Street has kept on providing me a good space for rest, chat and drink during my college days. The calm and cozy atmosphere of the bar in first floor of Badshah allowed me and my close friend to have tete-a-tete. It offered me and my friends to take a break from the hustle and bustle of Kolkata, talk among ourselves and plan what to do and what not to do. We had white rum with chicken fingers. The cost of per plate chicken fingers was Rs. 75.00. It was not so reasonable. We needed to pay a certain amount as VAT along with actual price of the dish, which eventually gave us a bit jerk and we felt that we could easily access a better quality restaurant in Esplanade area. Youngsters do love to visit Badshah for its exquisite kebabs, which are really yummy and chicken rolls. Badshah is famous for chicken rolls. Most people order chicken rolls and eat them up standing outside the restaurant. Even I sometimes opt for eating outside Badshah when I am too hungry to engulf one or two rolls at a time and do not have much time or money to move into a quality restaurant. Recently they are giving small quantities of chicken pieces inside rolls and the parathas have also got thin but the taste remains all the same. Mutton preparations are also great to eat in Badshah. The aloo and paneer rolls are not so great. They did not care to marinate the paneer pieces with the right kinds of spices that are essential for making vegetarian rolls. Among the main course meals I like Chicken Do Piyaza with rice. They are delicious and spicy to eat. That is the one main course of Badshah I cherish for till date. The surrounding of the ground floor, which happens to be the restaurant is too clumsy to sit and enjoy lunch with family.
