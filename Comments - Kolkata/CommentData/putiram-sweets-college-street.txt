This sweet shop is probably as old as the area itself, or so it seems. My father can well recount his days at the Presidency University, and how he and his friends would visit this place for an occasional rejuvenation. It has an absolutely unique and extremely delicious taste to it that makes you crave for more. The shop, as I said, looks old, and typical of the eateries around that place, accommodates people in long benches on either side of the table, all cluttered in as much space is available. However, the charm of the place and the cultural heritage it is a resemblance of will make you go back again and again. Also, try their sweets - especially the Roshogolla and Kalojam. They aren't the usual spongy ones that are available nowadays, but stay true to the original ones. Overall, drop in nearby just to be a part of the ancient food culture that it is a resemblance of.
